Boulder Startup Week 2026

A recommendation from Cody to Dan on how Caruso Ventures should show up at BSW this year — and why.

Starting from the top

What is this document about?

Every year, Boulder Startup Week (BSW) happens in Boulder, Colorado. It's a week-long event where startups, investors, and the tech community come together for talks, panels, and networking.

Caruso Ventures (that's us — "CV") has supported BSW before. Now the question is: How much should we invest this year?

This page walks through Cody's recommendation to Dan — what sponsorship level makes sense, why, and what else we should do beyond just writing a check.

What does "sponsorship" mean? When a company "sponsors" an event, it means they pay money to support it. In return, the sponsor usually gets their name/logo featured, speaking opportunities, or other perks. Think of it like how a company's logo appears on a sports jersey — they're paying to be associated with the event and support it.

Background

What is Boulder Startup Week?

Boulder Startup Week is a free, community-run event that happens every May in Boulder, Colorado. It's a week of talks, panels, workshops, parties, and pitch competitions — all focused on startups and innovation.

It's organized by Glider, a nonprofit that also runs TEDx Boulder. Each event under Glider raises its own budget independently, so BSW needs sponsors like us to fund itself each year.

The Recommendation

"Community Builder" ($1,000) + In-Kind Panels

What we're proposing

Pay $1,000 for the Community Builder sponsorship tier, and then host 2–3 panels during BSW week ourselves. These panels would cover topics close to our mission — things like:

  • Deep tech — cutting-edge technology companies
  • AI — artificial intelligence and its impact
  • Boulder's creative economy — arts, culture, and innovation
  • Founder-led storytelling — real stories from real entrepreneurs

We'd use our network to bring in high-caliber speakers — real founders, investors, and experts — to make these panels genuinely valuable for BSW attendees.

What does "in-kind" mean? "In-kind" support means contributing services or effort instead of (or alongside) money. Instead of just handing over cash, we're saying: "We'll also volunteer our time and network to make the event better." In this case, hosting panels = in-kind support. It's often more valuable than the check itself.

The Reasoning

Why This Level — And Not More?

1. Boulder Roots comes first

Boulder Roots is Caruso Ventures' flagship community initiative this year. It demands the largest share of our attention, budget, and team time. We need to be careful about spreading ourselves too thin.

Intelligence Gathered

What We Learned from BSW's Team

On February 11, 2026, we had a call with Holly and Melissa from the BSW/Glider team. Here's what we took away:

Growth is Real
Attendance grew from ~500 to ~4,000
BSW has seen 8x growth in 3 years. They expect 4,000–5,000 this year. The event is getting real traction.
But Ambition Felt Limited
The team has a "scarcity mindset"
Despite the growth, the BSW team hadn't fully imagined what's possible with more funding or bolder programming. They're thinking small relative to the opportunity.
Sponsorship is Evolving
New $20K top tier (up from $10K max)
They've updated their sponsorship levels and are still in early outreach. At the time of our call, they'd just gotten their first paid sponsor the day before.
Budget is Tight
Lean operation under the Glider nonprofit
Each Glider event raises its own money independently. The pitch competition prize pool (~$10K) is the main thing that flexes based on sponsor dollars.
Content Direction is Promising
They want: deep tech, real founder stories, creativity
All things that align with what we'd contribute. But the plan for how to get there still felt early and unformed.

Where BSW lands: Strong community values and alignment with us, but organizational execution and ambition could be higher. This is why we lead with content quality, not cash.
What does this tell us? BSW's heart is in the right place. The community values and content direction align perfectly with what Caruso Ventures cares about. But the team is still building its organizational muscle. This reinforces our strategy: contribute quality programming (which they need) rather than throwing more money at an organization still figuring out how to deploy it effectively.
