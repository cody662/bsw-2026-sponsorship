01 — Context

What This Is About

Every May, Boulder hosts a week-long celebration of its startup community. The question: how should Caruso Ventures participate?

Boulder Startup Week (BSW) is a free, community-run event — a week of talks, panels, workshops, and parties for founders, investors, and the broader tech community. It happens May 4–8, 2026.

It's run by Glider, a nonprofit that also organizes TEDx Boulder. Each event under Glider raises its own budget independently — meaning BSW needs sponsors like us to happen at all.

Caruso Ventures has supported BSW before. This document is Cody's recommendation to Dan on what level of support makes sense this year — balancing our desire to show up for the community with the reality of our other commitments.

What does "sponsorship" mean? When a company "sponsors" an event, they pay money to support it. In return, the sponsor usually gets their name featured, speaking opportunities, or other perks. Think of it like a company's logo on a sports jersey — they're paying to be associated with the event and help it happen.

03 — The Proposal

$1,000 Check +
High-Value Panels

Pay the entry-level sponsorship and then contribute what we're actually best at: curating excellent programming.

What We're Recommending

Select the Community Builder tier ($1,000) and supplement it with 2–3 panels during BSW week in areas that align with our mission:

  • Deep tech — cutting-edge technology companies
  • AI — artificial intelligence and its impact
  • Boulder's creative economy — arts, culture, and innovation
  • Founder-led storytelling — real stories from real entrepreneurs

We'd tap into CV's network to bring high-caliber speakers and produce sessions that genuinely elevate BSW's quality. We can also use BSW as a venue to promote Boulder Roots.

What does "in-kind" mean? "In-kind" support means contributing services or effort instead of — or alongside — money. Rather than just handing over cash, we're saying: "We'll also volunteer our time and network to make the event better." Hosting panels = in-kind support. Often more valuable than the check itself.

04 — The Reasoning

Why This Level —
And Not More?

1. Boulder Roots Takes Priority

Boulder Roots is our flagship community initiative this year. It demands the lion's share of our attention, budget, and team bandwidth. We need to be careful not to spread ourselves too thin.

2. We're Matching Last Year's Impact for Less

Here's a side-by-side of what we did in 2025 versus what's being proposed:

05 — Intelligence

What We Learned
from BSW's Team

On February 11, 2026, we spoke with Holly and Melissa from the BSW/Glider team. Here are the key takeaways:

Growth is Real
Attendance: 500 → 4,000 in three years
Solid trajectory. They expect 4,000–5,000 this year. The event has real traction in Boulder.
But Ambition Felt Limited
The team is thinking small relative to the opportunity
Despite growth, the BSW team still operates with a scarcity mindset. They hadn't fully envisioned what's possible with better funding or bolder programming.
Sponsorship Evolving
New $20K top tier (up from $10K max)
They've updated their sponsorship levels and were in early outreach — they'd just gotten their first paid sponsor the day before our call.
Budget is Lean
Each Glider event raises its own money
The pitch competition prize pool (~$10K total) is the main variable line item that flexes based on available sponsor dollars.
Content Direction Promising
They want: deep tech, founder stories, creativity
All things that align with what we'd want to contribute. But the execution plan for getting there still felt nascent and unformed.

BSW scores high on alignment with CV's values, but organizational ambition and execution lag behind. This is why we lead with content quality, not cash.
Strategic takeaway BSW's heart is in the right place — the community values and content direction align well with what Caruso Ventures cares about. But the organizational capacity and vision don't match the opportunity. This reinforces our approach: contribute quality programming (which they clearly need) rather than throw more money at an organization still figuring out how to deploy it effectively.
